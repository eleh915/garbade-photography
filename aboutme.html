RACHEL GARBADE
travel contrast portraits dance about me

Right now I am located somewhere between Short Hills, New Jersey, Poughkeepsie, New York, and the rest of the world. Over the past few years I have traveled from Maine to New Mexico, from Israel to Italy, from Austin to Avignon, and everywhere in between. I’ve lived in apartments in Paris and New York City, dorm rooms in Poughkeepsie, and my childhood bedroom in Short Hills. At Vassar College, I am currently completing my Degree of Bachelor of Arts in Art History and French, where I plan to graduate in May of 2015.

To me, photography is not a hobby, but rather a way of life. When I’m walking to an art history seminar, eating lunch with friends, at a dress rehearsal for Vassar Repertory Dance Theater, on a photo shoot for Contrast, Vassar’s art and style magazine, gazing out the window, or traveling somewhere far away, my camera is always nearby. On January 1, 2013 I started a project where I would take at least one photo every single day for a year. As I’m writing this it is January 2015 and the project is still going on – without a single day missed. Needless to say, my camera has become something of an auxiliary appendage.

Over the years, photography has ultimately become a way for me to share what I find beautiful with those around me. Every time I open the shutter on my camera – whether its digital, analog, Polaroid, or on my iPhone – I have the specific goal of capturing one singular moment in my busy life that made me stop in my tracks with the hopes of evoking that response in someone else.
